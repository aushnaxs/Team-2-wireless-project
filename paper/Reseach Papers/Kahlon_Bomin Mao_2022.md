# Paper Summary 1

## Paper Title: AI Models for Green Communications Towards 6G
## AUTHOR
Mao, B., Tang, F., Kawamoto, Y., & Kato, N. (2022). *AI Models for Green Communications Towards 6G*. IEEE Communications Surveys & Tutorials, 24(1), 210–247. https://export.arxiv.org/pdf/2101.01588v2

## Summary
This paper examines how artificial intelligence (AI) can make wireless networks more energy-efficient as they move toward 6G. The authors discuss the rising energy demands caused by the rapid growth of devices and network infrastructure. They review machine learning and deep learning methods for power management, network optimization, and energy harvesting to reduce energy use. The paper also highlights challenges such as maintaining service quality and flexibility while improving efficiency, and outlines future research directions for AI in green 6G communications.  

## Relation to Team’s Topic
This paper supports the “AI for green networks” side of the sustainability paradox. It shows how AI can improve energy efficiency in networks, which is a key aspect of our project evaluating the trade-offs between AI-driven network optimization and its environmental impact.  

## Critical Comment
While the paper thoroughly reviews methods for reducing network energy use, it does not examine the environmental cost of training or operating AI models, such as electricity consumption and carbon emissions, which is an essential part of the sustainability paradox.
