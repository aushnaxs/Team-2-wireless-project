## Paper Summary
## Paper Title: Overview of AI and Communication for 6G Network: Fundamentals, Challenges, and Future Research Opportunities
## Authors: Qimei Cui et al.
## Year: 2025
## DOI: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11432-024-4337-1
## 1. Problem
   
As communication systems move toward 6G, networks are expected to handle massive data, ultra-low latency, and more intelligent services. Traditional approaches are not enough to meet these demands efficiently. The paper looks at how integrating AI into communication systems can solve these challenges, while also introducing new issues related to complexity and resource use.

## 2. Approach

The authors break down the integration of AI and 6G into three main stages:
AI for network: using AI to improve performance and efficiency
Network for AI: designing networks that better support AI operations
AI as a service: embedding AI capabilities directly into network services
They also discuss system design, architecture, and how AI can be applied across different layers of the network.

## 3. Key Findings

AI can significantly improve network efficiency, resource allocation, and overall performance.
Future 6G systems will rely heavily on AI at multiple levels.
However, this integration increases system complexity and requires more computational resources.
The paper highlights ongoing challenges and the need for more efficient and scalable AI solutions.

## 4. Relevance to Our Topic

This paper directly supports the idea of the sustainability paradox. It shows that AI can make networks more efficient and intelligent, but also increases computational demands and energy use. This reflects the core trade-off in our project between AI improving network sustainability and AI contributing to higher energy consumption.
