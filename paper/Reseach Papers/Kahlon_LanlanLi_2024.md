## Paper Summary
## Paper Title: Environmental Impact of AI in Next-Generation Networks
## Authors: Lanlan Li
## Year: 2024
## URL: Research on future 6G green wireless networks - ScienceDirect https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2949736124000836

## 1. Problem
   
As AI becomes more integrated into next-generation networks like 5G and 6G, there is growing concern about its environmental impact. While AI can improve network efficiency, it also requires large-scale data processing, which increases energy consumption and carbon emissions. The paper looks at this trade-off between performance and sustainability.

## 2. Approach
   
The authors analyze how AI is used in modern telecom networks and evaluate both its benefits and environmental costs. They review different AI-driven techniques such as network optimization, traffic prediction, and resource allocation. The paper also examines the energy usage of AI models and supporting infrastructure like data centers.

## 3. Key Findings
   
AI can significantly improve network efficiency by reducing unnecessary energy usage.
However, training and running AI models requires high computational power, increasing overall energy demand.
Data centers and telecom infrastructure contribute heavily to carbon emissions.
Sustainable solutions like energy-efficient algorithms and green data centers are necessary to balance this impact.

## 4. Relevance to Our Topic
   
This paper directly supports the idea of the “Sustainability Paradox.” It shows that while AI helps create greener and more efficient networks, it also adds to environmental problems due to its high energy needs. This makes it highly relevant for analyzing both the positive and negative impacts of AI in green networking.
