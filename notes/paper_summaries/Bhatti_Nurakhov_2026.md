

## Paper Summary: The Impact of Generative AI on 6G Network Architecture and Service 
Authors: Yedil Nurakhov, Serik Aibagarov, Nurislam Kassymbek, Aksultan Mukhanbet, Bolatzhan Kumalakov, and Timur Imankulov 
Year: 2026

### 1. Problem
This source addresses the challenges of transition form 5G to 6G which requires managing hyper-heterogeneous networks and overcoming the Shannon capacity limit. Current models face a challenge which is struggling to adapt to unpredictable mobile traffic. As well as technicians and engineers are finding it hard to translate high level service goals into low level configurations in wireless components. 

### 2. Approach
The authors analyse categories from 2019 – 2025 and categorize the impact of generative ai and large language models on 6G. 

1.	Architecture
2.	Management 
3.	Security
4.	Semantics
5.	Edge AI

### 3. Key Findings
6G will have AI Agents that act like self generative AI for the internet, they can read thousands of pages of technical manuals to fix a problem.  However, running on of these big AI models can take extreme electricity. If every cell tower needed it own AI agent, the power bill will rise. Also, inside of sending a massive file, 6G will use AI to send a sketch when then will let AI reconstruct the rest which can cut data traffic by 90%. However there is a safety risk such as GenAI may generate invalid configurations. 

###4. Relevance
The paper is relevant because it provides a deeper understanding of the technical aspect of 6G and how  this is a sustainability paradox.
