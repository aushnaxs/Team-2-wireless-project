## Paper Summary: Making AI Less ‘Thirsty’: Uncovering and addressing the secret water footprint of AI models


Authors: Pengfei Li, Jianyi Yang, Mohammad A. Islam, and Shaolei Ren 
Year: 2025

### 1. Problem
This paper addresses the rapid increase of usage of water for the footprint of AI models, which has largely been overlooked. As AI products expand, datacenter water consumption is escalating. With some companies with an increase of 34%. The lack of acknowledgement of this concern has let companies be untruthful within their reports. 

### 2. Approach
The authors proposed a principled methodology to estimates AI’s total water footprint by categorizing into three scopes. 
1.	Onsite - Water consumed for cooling datacenters  
2.	Offsite – Water used by power plants to generate the electricity that powers the data centers. 
3.	Embodied – Water used to manufacture AI chips and servers 


### 3. Key Findings
Training GPT – 3 in Microsoft U.S. datacentres is estimated to consume 5.3 million litters of water. Global Ai water withdrawal is projected to reach 4.2-6.6. billion cubic meters by 2027. 


### 4. Relevance to Our Topic
This paper is relevant to our topic (Energy-Hungry AI) since its expands on the AI sustainability which goes beyond electricity and carbon to include the physical impact on local water resources.
